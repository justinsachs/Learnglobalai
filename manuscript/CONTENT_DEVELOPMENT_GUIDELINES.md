## Content Quality Standards

### Word Count Requirements

**Per Major Heading (e.g., 1.1, 1.2, 1.3):**
- Minimum: 1,500 words
- Target: 1,500-2,500 words of substantive content

**Per Module/Chapter:**
- Minimum: 5,000 words
- Target: 5,000-8,000 words

### Source Material Requirements

**Per Major Section:**
- Minimum: 3-5 distinct, validated sources
- Must include multiple perspectives
- Sources must be credible and recent (prefer 2020-2026)
- Mix of academic research, industry reports, and practitioner insights

### Visual Requirements

**Per Major Section:**
- Minimum: 1 supporting visual element
- Types: infographic, chart, table, diagram, or process flow
- Must directly support and clarify the content
- Must be validated against source material

## Chapter Structure Template

Each chapter MUST follow this 6-part structure:

### 1. Cold Open Story (1 page / ~300-400 words)
- A learner failure or organizational pain point
- Real-world scenario that illustrates the problem
- Sets emotional and practical stakes

### 2. The Principle (~500-800 words)
- What's broken in current approaches
- What replaces it
- Why this matters now (AI context)

### 3. The Model (~600-1,000 words)
- One diagram or framework (repeatable mental model)
- Visual representation of the concept
- Clear explanation of how the model works

### 4. The Playbook (~1,500-2,500 words)
- Step-by-step how-to (operator instructions)
- Actionable, specific guidance
- Can be implemented immediately

### 5. Failure Modes (~500-800 words)
- What goes wrong
- Common mistakes and pitfalls
- How to prevent or recover from failures

### 6. Proof Task (~300-500 words)
- Short exercise or checklist
- Reader can run immediately
- Validates understanding and application

## Sub-Topic Depth Checklist

For each sub-topic, ensure coverage of:

1. **Definition/Explanation** - What is it?
2. **Context/Importance** - Why does it matter?
3. **How-To Procedures** - Step-by-step implementation
4. **Common Mistakes/Pitfalls** - What to avoid
5. **Best Practices** - What works well
6. **Real-World Examples** - Concrete scenarios
7. **Connections** - Links to other modules/concepts

## Content Development Workflow

### Step A: Compile Research
- Gather 3-5 distinct validated sources per major section
- Document key insights, statistics, and metrics
- Identify multiple perspectives and viewpoints
- Save all sources with proper citations

### Step B: Write the Content
- Follow chapter template structure
- Adhere to founding parameters (action vs theory, technical vs conceptual)
- Meet minimum word count requirements (1,500-2,500 per heading)
- Write substantive, non-repetitive content

### Step C: Edit and Review
- Compare content against full outline
- Check for repetition with other sections
- Identify content that belongs in other sections
- Relocate misplaced content

### Step D: Add Additional Content
- Ensure adequate depth using Sub-Topic Depth Checklist
- Expand thin sections
- Add examples, case studies, and practical applications
- Avoid being long-winded or repetitive

### Step D.5: Depth Check (NEW VALIDATION GATE)
Before creating visuals, confirm for each sub-heading:
- Is this topic explained thoroughly enough?
- Could a reader with no prior knowledge understand AND execute?
- If not, expand before proceeding

### Step E: Create Visuals
- Create at least one supporting visual per major section
- Types: infographic, chart, graph, diagram, process flow
- Ensure visual clarity and professional quality

### Step F: Validate Graphics
- Fact-check graphic/chart against source material
- Confirm alignment with content message
- Ensure visual accuracy and clarity

### Step G: Place in Manuscript
- Integrate content into full manuscript
- Ensure proper formatting and flow
- Maintain consistent voice and style

### Step H: Begin Next Section
- Move to next section of outline
- Repeat process

## Repetition Prevention Protocol

### During Writing (Step C)
- Review content against all previously written sections
- Identify overlapping concepts
- Ensure unique angle or perspective
- If repetitive, either:
  - Reframe with different emphasis
  - Move to more appropriate section
  - Delete if truly redundant

### After Each Chapter
- Run full-document repetition check
- Identify repeated phrases, concepts, or examples
- Consolidate or differentiate as needed

### After Full Draft
- Comprehensive content audit
- Identify weak areas needing development
- Identify areas with too much repetition
- Flag sections lacking sufficient depth

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Voice and Tone
- Professional but accessible
- Operator-focused (practical, not academic)
- Confident and authoritative
- Direct and clear

### Formatting
- Use complete paragraphs (not bullet-heavy)
- Alternate between prose and tables
- Use bold for key concepts
- Use blockquotes for definitions or notable excerpts
- Include inline citations with reference-style links

### Avoid
- Excessive jargon without explanation
- Vague generalizations
- Repetitive phrasing
- Academic fluff
- Buzzwords without substance

## The ADAPT Framework Integration

Every chapter should reference or apply the ADAPT framework where relevant:

- **A — Audit the learner reality:** starting level, constraints, motivation, context, risk
- **D — Define outcomes as proofs:** not "understand," but what success looks like and how it's verified
- **A — Assemble source truth:** the canonical knowledge base (manuals, standards, SMEs, data)
- **P — Personalize the path:** AI tutor + branching modules + pacing + modality choices
- **T — Test for transfer:** performance tasks, simulations, field verification, and feedback loops
