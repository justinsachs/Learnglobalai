# Evidence-Based Credentials vs. Digital Badges Research

## The Problem with Digital Badges

### Forbes (October 2024)

**Key Finding:** "Based on a new report from UpSkill America, a project of The Aspen Institute, **digital badges are still highly confusing and of very limited value**."[^1]

### Accredible (May 2021)

**Security Concerns:** "Digital credentials contain recipient details which can cause some concern around **data protection**."[^2]

### Medium (Noah Geisel)

**Verification Issues:** "Colleges and employers have a problem: many of the skills they seek are **not represented on transcripts and difficult or impossible to verify** on..."[^3]

## The Shift to Verifiable, Evidence-Based Credentials

### 1EdTech (March 2025)

**Inflection Point:** "The need for digital credentials to support student success, highlight the value of educational institutions, and **meet employer demands is no longer questioned**."[^4]

### Credly

**Trustworthy and Verifiable:** "Trustworthy and recognized digital credentials act as **verifiable proof of an individual's skills and achievements**, creating a solid foundation for professional..."[^5]

### ACT

**Evidence-Based:** "Evidence-based assessments are supported by **validity evidence accumulated through scientific theory and research**."[^6]

## Key Takeaways for Book

1.  **Digital Badges Are Not Enough:** The market is flooded with low-quality, unverified digital badges. They are confusing to employers and have limited value.
2.  **The Market Demands Proof:** Employers do not want more credentials. They want reliable signals of competence. They want proof.
3.  **The Future is Verifiable and Evidence-Based:** The new currency of the labor market is the verifiable credential—a claim of competence that is backed by a rich portfolio of evidence.
4.  **The Competence Ledger is the Ultimate Credential:** The Competence Ledger is the ultimate verifiable credential. It is a secure, auditable, and comprehensive record of everything a learner can do, backed by a portfolio of proof.
