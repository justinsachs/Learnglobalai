# Transfer of Learning and Authentic Assessment Research

## Transfer of Learning Effectiveness

### Wilson Learning - Learning Transfer Model

**Key Finding:** "Addressing Learner Readiness can increase the effectiveness of learning transfer by as much as **70%**."

**Comprehensive Impact:** "The impact of learning can be enhanced by as much as **186%** when all of the learning transfer methods are utilized."

**Implication:** Transfer is not automatic—it must be designed and supported. But when done well, it dramatically improves outcomes.

**Source:** Wilson Learning. "Learning Transfer Model." https://global.wilsonlearning.com/resources/learning-transfer/

### PMC (2025) - HosseiniKhezri et al.

**Study Focus:** Learning in Physical Education: A Transfer of Learning Theory Perspective

**Key Finding:** "A successful transfer occurs when the learner **transcends knowledge and skills learned to enhance success in further learning** or [real-world application]."

**Citation Count:** Cited by 3 papers

**Implication:** Transfer is not just about repeating what was learned—it is about applying it in new contexts.

**Citation:** HosseiniKhezri, A., et al. (2025). "Learning in Physical Education: A Transfer of Learning Theory Perspective." PMC. [Cited by 3 papers] https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12629222/

### Taylor & Francis (2025) - Kauffeld et al.

**Study Focus:** Learning and transfer in organisations: how it works

**Key Finding:** Examines how learning and transfer in organizations function and how these processes can be supported.

**Citation Count:** Cited by 19 papers

**Implication:** Transfer is not just an educational concept—it is a critical organizational capability.

**Citation:** Kauffeld, S., et al. (2025). "Learning and transfer in organisations: how it works." Taylor & Francis. [Cited by 19 papers] https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1359432X.2025.2463799

### Yale Poorvu Center

**Definition:** "Transfer is the ability to apply learned knowledge or skills to new contexts and is a **key indicator of deep understanding**."

**Implication:** If learners cannot transfer, they do not deeply understand—they have only memorized.

**Source:** Yale Poorvu Center for Teaching and Learning. "Transfer of Knowledge to New Contexts." https://poorvucenter.yale.edu/transfer-of-knowledge-to-new-contexts

### ScienceDirect (2022) - Mohajerzad et al.

**Study Focus:** A scoping review about transfer strategies in the field of education

**Key Finding:** Transfer strategies may include activities to identify research questions that are relevant for everyday teaching and learning, to communicate research findings effectively.

**Citation Count:** Cited by 24 papers

**Implication:** Transfer strategies must be explicitly designed—they do not happen automatically.

**Citation:** Mohajerzad, H., et al. (2022). "A scoping review about transfer strategies in the field of education." ScienceDirect. [Cited by 24 papers] https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666557322000398

## Near Transfer vs. Far Transfer

### NIFDI (September 2019) - Hempenstall

**Definitions:**
- **Near transfer:** Skills learned in one context are applied to **similar contexts**
- **Far transfer:** Skills are applied to contexts that are **significantly different**

**Key Insight:** Near transfer is easier and more reliable. Far transfer is harder but more valuable.

**Source:** NIFDI (September 25, 2019). "Near and far transfer in cognitive training." https://www.nifdi.org/resources/hempenstall-blog/758-near-and-far-transfer-in-cognitive-training.html

### Learning Science Communication (November 2020)

**Key Distinction:**
- **Near Transfer (easier / more similar):** Transfer to similar concepts with overlapping elements
- **Far Transfer (harder / less similar):** Transfer to less similar concepts, highly dependent on schemata and background knowledge

**Implication:** Far transfer is more likely if learners have strong conceptual understanding and organized knowledge structures.

**Source:** Learning Science Communication (November 25, 2020). "Transfer - Near and Far: An important idea for all teachers to understand." https://www.learningscicomm.com/post/transfer-near-and-far-an-important-idea-for-all-teachers-to-understand

### Wikipedia - Transfer of Learning

**Near Transfer Definition:** "Near transfer occurs when **many elements overlap** between the conditions in which the learner obtained the knowledge or skill and the new situation."

**Far Transfer Definition:** "Far transfer occurs when the conditions are **significantly different** from the original learning context."

**Source:** Wikipedia. "Transfer of learning." https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transfer_of_learning

### Roger Williams University

**Example of Far Transfer:** "Learning to play chess and then applying strategic thinking to business decisions."

**Key Principle:** Far transfer requires **abstract understanding** of principles, not just procedural knowledge.

**Source:** Roger Williams University. "Transfer – Thinking Rhetorically: Writing for Professional Contexts." https://rwu.pressbooks.pub/thinkingrhetorically/chapter/transfer/

### University of Delaware - Definitions of Transfer

**Definitions:**
- **Near transfer:** Transfer between very similar but not identical contexts
- **Far transfer:** Transfer between contexts that, on appearance, seem remote and alien

**Implication:** The distance between learning context and application context determines difficulty of transfer.

**Source:** University of Delaware. "Definitions of Transfer when learning in one context." https://www1.udel.edu/dssep/transfer/Definitions%20of%20Transfer.pdf

## Authentic and Performance-Based Assessment

### Maverick Learning Press

**Definition:** "Authentic Assessment is a form of assessment in which students are asked to perform **real-world tasks** that demonstrate meaningful application of essential knowledge and skills."

**Key Principle:** Assessment should mirror real-world performance, not just academic exercises.

**Source:** Maverick Learning Press. "Authentic/Performance Assessment." https://mlpp.pressbooks.pub/mavlearn/chapter/authentic-performance-assessment/

### University of Illinois Chicago (April 2022)

**Key Insight:** "Authentic assessments can also be referred to as **alternative assessments** or **performance-based assessments**."

**All three terms refer to:** Assessments that require demonstration of competence in realistic contexts.

**Source:** University of Illinois Chicago (April 15, 2022). "Authentic Assessments." https://teaching.uic.edu/cate-teaching-guides/assessment-grading-practices/authentic-assessments/

### ASCD - Meyer

**Key Distinction:** "The ability to use the criteria to determine the quality of students' work is what we mean by **expert judgment**, and it is far from the subjective process."

**Implication:** Authentic assessment requires clear criteria (rubrics) to make expert judgment reliable and defensible.

**Source:** ASCD. "What's the Difference Between Authentic and Performance Assessment?" https://files.ascd.org/staticfiles/ascd/pdf/journals/ed_lead/el_199205_meyer.pdf

### University of Connecticut

**Definition:** "Authentic assessment is a form of assessment in which students demonstrate **meaningful application of knowledge and skills** by performing real-world tasks."

**Key Principle:** "Meaningful application" means the task matters in the real world, not just in the classroom.

**Source:** University of Connecticut CETL. "Alternative Authentic Assessment Methods." https://cetl.uconn.edu/resources/teaching-and-learning-assessment/teaching-and-learning-assessment-overview/assessment-design/alternative-authentic-assessment-methods/

## Transfer Theories and Instructional Practices

### ERIC - Transfer Theories Review

**Study Focus:** Review of transfer theories and effective instructional practices

**Key Theories Covered:**
- Theory of identical elements
- Low and high road transfer
- Analogy
- Situated learning

**Implication:** Multiple theories exist, but all emphasize that transfer must be explicitly designed and supported.

**Source:** ERIC. "Transfer of learning and teaching: A review of transfer theories and effective instructional practices." https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1217940

### High Fidelity Simulation Study

**Study Focus:** High fidelity simulation effectiveness in nursing students' transfer of learning

**Key Finding:** High fidelity simulation facilitates learning transfer. Use of **performance scores** reinforces evidence of effectiveness.

**Implication:** Realistic simulations improve transfer to real-world performance.

**Source:** "High fidelity simulation effectiveness in nursing students' transfer of learning." De Gruyter.

### Case Study Method for Transfer

**Study Focus:** Use of case study method to increase near and far transfer of learning

**Key Distinction:**
- **Near transfer:** Application of learning to situations similar to those in which initial learning took place
- **Far transfer:** Application to significantly different situations

**Finding:** Case study method can increase both near and far transfer.

**Source:** "Use of case study method to increase near and far transfer of learning." Wiley Online Library.

### Salomon & Perkins - Transfer of Learning

**Key Insight:** Problem isomorphs (structurally identical problems) represent near transfer because they are nearly identical structurally.

**Finding:** Students acquired **transferable tendencies to self-monitor and self-direct**—not just specific procedures.

**Implication:** Transfer of metacognitive skills (how to learn) is more powerful than transfer of specific content.

**Source:** Salomon, G., & Perkins, D. "Transfer of Learning." ResearchGate.

## Key Takeaways for Book

### Transfer Effectiveness

1. **70% Improvement:** Addressing learner readiness increases transfer effectiveness by 70% (Wilson Learning)

2. **186% Enhancement:** Using all transfer methods enhances learning impact by 186% (Wilson Learning)

3. **Key Indicator of Deep Understanding:** Transfer is the key indicator that learners deeply understand, not just memorize (Yale)

4. **Organizational Capability:** Transfer is critical organizational capability, not just educational concept (19 citations, Kauffeld)

5. **Must Be Designed:** Transfer strategies must be explicitly designed—they do not happen automatically (24 citations, Mohajerzad)

### Near vs. Far Transfer

6. **Near Transfer Easier:** Occurs when many elements overlap between learning and application contexts

7. **Far Transfer Harder:** Requires abstract understanding of principles and strong conceptual knowledge

8. **Far Transfer More Valuable:** Applying strategic thinking from chess to business is far transfer—harder but more impactful

9. **Schemata Dependent:** Far transfer highly dependent on organized knowledge structures and background knowledge

### Authentic Assessment

10. **Real-World Tasks Required:** Authentic assessment requires performing real-world tasks, not just academic exercises

11. **Meaningful Application:** Tasks must matter in the real world, not just in the classroom

12. **Expert Judgment with Rubrics:** Authentic assessment requires clear criteria (rubrics) to make judgment reliable

13. **Performance-Based:** Also called alternative assessment or performance-based assessment—all emphasize demonstration

### Transfer Theories

14. **Multiple Theories Converge:** Identical elements, low/high road transfer, analogy, situated learning—all emphasize explicit design

15. **Metacognitive Transfer Most Powerful:** Transfer of self-monitoring and self-direction more powerful than transfer of specific content

16. **Simulation Facilitates Transfer:** High fidelity simulation improves transfer to real-world performance

17. **Case Studies Increase Transfer:** Case study method can increase both near and far transfer

### Strategic Implications

18. **Transfer Is the Metric:** Completion and quiz scores do not matter—transfer to real-world performance is the only metric that matters

19. **Learner Readiness Critical:** Must prepare learners for transfer—cannot assume it will happen automatically

20. **Novel Contexts Required:** Transfer tasks must present novel scenarios, not repetition of practiced examples

21. **Evidence Portfolios:** Performance artifacts document transfer and make credentials defensible

22. **Field Verification:** Ultimate test of transfer is performance in the real-world environment, not just simulation
