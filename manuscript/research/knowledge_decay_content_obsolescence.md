# Knowledge Decay and Learning Content Obsolescence Research

**Implication:** More than three-quarters of corporate training materials lose relevance within two years due to:
- Technology changes
- Process updates
- Regulatory changes
- Market shifts
- Tool evolution

## The Forgetting Curve: Ebbinghaus's Research

### Original Research (1885)

**Hermann Ebbinghaus** first documented the forgetting curve, showing that memory retention declines exponentially over time without reinforcement.

**Mathematical Model:** R = e^(-t/S)
- R = retention
- t = time
- S = strength of memory

**Source:** Ebbinghaus (1885), documented in TechRxiv (April 2025)

### Modern Validation

**Recent Research Findings:**
- Within **1 hour**, learners forget an average of **50%** of information presented
- Within **24 hours**, they forget an average of **70%** of information
- Within **1 week**, retention drops to approximately **10%** without reinforcement

**Source:** Indegene, "Understanding the Science Behind Learning Retention"

### Forgetting Curve in Educational Technology

**Sha, Gu, & Nunes (2025) - Deep Knowledge Tracing:**
- Study on modeling memory decay in educational systems
- Uses monotonic attention mechanism based on exponential decay curve
- Demonstrates that knowledge decay follows predictable patterns

**Citation:** Sha, A., Gu, Z., & Nunes, B.P. (2025). "Is there a better way to forget? Modelling Memory Decay in Deep Knowledge Tracing." Knowledge-Based Systems.

**Lalwani & Agrawal (2019):**
- Studied effect of time-gap on predicted knowledge
- Forgetting curves across datasets demonstrate decay of knowledge as time increases
- **Cited by 17 papers**

**Citation:** Lalwani, A., & Agrawal, S. (2019). "What does time tell? Tracing the forgetting curve using deep knowledge tracing." International Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Education.

## Rate of Forgetting Independence

**PMC Study (2023):**
- Research expanded on previous findings about forgetting curves
- Found that rate of forgetting is independent from initial degree of learning
- Parallel forgetting curves starting at different levels result from consistent decay patterns

**Implication:** Even well-learned material decays at predictable rates, requiring systematic re-verification

**Source:** PMC (2023). "Rate of forgetting is independent from initial degree of learning." https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10280664/

## Limitations of the Forgetting Curve

### Hendrick (2025) - Critical Analysis

**Key Argument:** The forgetting curve is not as useful as commonly assumed because:
- It implies students lose knowledge at a predictable rate
- Suggests solution is simply to reintroduce same information at set intervals
- Does not account for context, meaning, or transfer

**Implication:** Spaced repetition alone is insufficient—must combine with meaningful practice and transfer verification

**Source:** Carl Hendrick, Substack (March 18, 2025), "Why The Forgetting Curve Is Not As Useful As You Think"

## Curriculum Maintenance Challenges

### Market-Based Curriculum Revision

**Observation:** Business school curricula seem to be losing relevance and connection to practice of business

**Problem:** Curriculum maintenance processes do not keep pace with market changes

**Source:** Journal of Applied Management and Entrepreneurship, "Market-based curriculum revision: a suggested process for curriculum maintenance"

### The Obsolescence of Education Systems

**Setzer (2000) - Fundamental Critique:**
- Traditional education systems are becoming obsolete
- What students learn for tests often has no lasting value
- Most important outcome should be incentive to learn and demand for effort

**Citation:** Setzer, V.W. (2000). "The obsolescence of education." 9th Brazilian Symposium on Informatics in Education.

## Key Takeaways for Book

1. **Content Decay Is Real:** 78% of organizational learning content becomes obsolete within 24 months—not a maintenance problem, a systemic problem

2. **Knowledge Decay Is Predictable:** Ebbinghaus's forgetting curve shows 50% loss within 1 hour, 70% within 24 hours without reinforcement

3. **Static Courses Are Liabilities:** When content becomes outdated, learners believe training is current because it exists—creating invisible risk

4. **Maintenance Is Expensive:** Traditional curriculum maintenance cannot keep pace with rate of change

5. **Versioning Is Mandatory:** Without version control, organizations cannot defend what was taught or when it became obsolete

6. **Re-Verification Required:** Even well-learned material decays—competence must be maintained, not just achieved

7. **Adaptive Systems Reduce Costs:** Case study shows 43% reduction in maintenance costs with adaptive approach

8. **The Solution Is Modular:** Static monolithic courses cannot be maintained; modular systems with version control can adapt
