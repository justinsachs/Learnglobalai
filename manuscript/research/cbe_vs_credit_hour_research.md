# Competency-Based Education vs. Credit Hour Research

## The Problem with the Credit Hour

### C-BEN (August 2025)

**Key Insight:** "Let's start with the elephant in the room: the **credit hour is a fundamentally flawed and artificial construct with no relationship to learning**."[^1]

### The EvoLLLution (January 2019)

**The Challenge:** While it seems simple to just change from credit hours to competencies, the entire system of higher education is built around the credit hour, making the transition complex.[^2]

## Competency-Based Education (CBE) as the Alternative

### PNPI (April 2023)

**Definition:** "Competency-based education (CBE) programs are a **pedagogical alternative to credit hours** in higher education. Seen by proponents as a greater alignment to [the skills needed for the workforce]."[^3]

### EDUCAUSE

**Core Principle:** "The competency-based education (CBE) approach allows students to **advance based on their ability to master a skill or competency at their own pace** regardless of time, place, or pace of learning."[^4]

### KnowledgeWorks (November 2023)

**Key Benefit:** "Competency-based education (CBE) is a proven model that **accelerates learning by building on students' skills** and focuses on the competencies they need for success."[^5]

## The Shift to Proof of Skills

### The Journal (October 2022)

**The Next Phase:** "Carnegie units and requisite seat time began to fade. The next phase must be a **shift from courses and grades to skills and evidence**."[^6]

### Getting Smart (November 2025)

**New Signals:** "They connect learners to opportunity not through degrees or seat time, but through **demonstrated capabilities and authentic experiences**."[^7]

### Training Industry

**Core of Adaptive Learning:** "The idea of measuring learning by mastery of a subject, rather than by seat time, lies at the core of adaptive learning."[^8]

## Key Takeaways for Book

1.  **The Credit Hour is Obsolete:** The credit hour is a measure of time, not learning. It is a fundamentally flawed construct that is holding back innovation in higher education.
2.  **CBE is the Alternative:** Competency-based education (CBE) offers a pedagogical alternative that is focused on mastery, not seat time.
3.  **From Seat Time to Proof of Skill:** The future of credentials is not about how long you sat in a chair, but what you can prove you can do.
4.  **Proof Hours, Not Credit Hours:** The new unit of value is the "Proof Hour"—a measure of verified competence, backed by a portfolio of evidence.
