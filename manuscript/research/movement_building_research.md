## Communities of Practice

### Wenger-Trayner

**Definition:** "Communities of practice are **groups of people who share a concern or a passion for something they do and learn how to do it better as they interact regularly**."[^4]

### Forbes (January 2024)

**Why You Need One:** "A community of practice is any group of people with a shared interest who, by interacting, learn from one another and hone their abilities."[^5]

### The Systems Thinker

**Learning as a Social System:** "Communities of practice develop around things that matter to people. As a result, their practices reflect the members' own understanding of what is important."[^6]

## Diffusion of Innovations

### Everett Rogers

**The Theory:** "Diffusion is the process by which an **innovation is communicated through certain channels over time among the members of a social system**."[^7]

### Wikipedia

**Key Elements:** The four main elements in diffusion of innovations are the innovation itself, communication channels, time, and a social system.[^8]

## Key Takeaways for Book

1.  **A Movement, Not a Mandate:** You cannot force people to change. You have to inspire them to want to change. This is the work of building a movement.
2.  **Find the Early Adopters:** Every movement starts with a small group of passionate, committed people—the early adopters. Find them, empower them, and they will spread the word.
3.  **Create a Community of Practice:** A movement needs a home. A community of practice is a place where people can come together to learn from each other, to support each other, and to co-create the future.
4.  **The Power of a Shared Vision:** A movement needs a clear and compelling vision of the future. It needs a story that people can believe in and a cause that they are willing to fight for.
5.  **It Starts with You:** Every movement starts with a single person who is willing to stand up and say, “There is a better way.” You can be that person.
