## Misconception Detection

### arXiv (February 2026) - Mitton et al.

**Novel Approach:** Presents a new method for **detecting misconceptions from student-tutor dialogues** using large language models (LLMs).[^4]

### ACM Digital Library (2023) - Jell et al.

**Method:** Describes methods for **detecting misconceptions using Abstract Syntax Trees (AST)** and providing tailored, level-specific feedback.[^5]

### ResearchGate - Data-Driven Discovery

**Technique:** Investigates a **data-driven technique to discover students' misconceptions** in interactions with constraint-based Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITSs).[^6]

**Application:** "The Socratic Mind is a scalable, AI-powered oral assessment platform leveraging **Socratic questioning to challenge students to explain, justify, and defend**..."[^8]

**Focus:** Investigates how AI-powered tools, when integrated thoughtfully, can **enhance critical thinking through the Socratic questioning method**.[^9]

## Key Takeaways for Book

1.  **AI Coaching is Scalable & Personalized:** AI coaches make personalized coaching accessible to thousands of learners simultaneously.
2.  **Misconception Detection is Key:** A critical function of an AI tutor is to detect not just *what* a learner got wrong, but *why*.
3.  **Data-Driven Discovery:** Misconceptions can be discovered by analyzing patterns in learner interaction data.
4.  **Socratic Dialogue Drives Deeper Learning:** Instead of giving answers, the AI tutor should ask questions that force the learner to confront their own thinking.
5.  **Explain, Justify, Defend:** The goal of Socratic dialogue is to challenge the learner to explain their reasoning, justify their conclusions, and defend their position.
