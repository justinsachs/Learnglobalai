## Key Takeaways for Book

1.  **The Surveillance Economy Comes to Education:** The same business model that powers social media—collecting vast amounts of personal data to predict and influence behavior—is now being applied to education.
2.  **Personalization vs. Surveillance:** There is a fine line between personalization and surveillance. We need to find ways to personalize learning without violating student privacy and dignity.
3.  **The Data is the Learner:** The data that is collected about a learner is not just data. It is a representation of their thoughts, their struggles, their aspirations. It must be treated with respect.
4.  **Privacy-Preserving AI is Possible:** Techniques like federated learning and differential privacy allow us to train AI models on decentralized data without ever having to collect the raw data in a central location.
5.  **The Competence Ledger is a Privacy-Enhancing Technology:** By giving learners ownership and control over their own data, the Competence Ledger is a powerful tool for protecting student privacy.
