# Learning Objectives and Rubric Design Research

## Bloom's Taxonomy and Learning Objectives

### PMC Study (2015) - Adams

**Key Finding:** Bloom's taxonomy differentiates between cognitive skill levels and calls attention to learning objectives that require **higher levels of cognitive skills**.

**Implication:** Not all learning objectives are equal—some require recall, others require application, analysis, or creation.

**Citation:** Adams, N.E. (2015). "Bloom's taxonomy of cognitive learning objectives." PMC. [Cited by 1,783 papers] https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4511057/

### Bloom's Taxonomy Levels (Revised)

**Six Levels of Cognitive Skills:**

1. **Remember:** Recall facts and basic concepts
2. **Understand:** Explain ideas or concepts
3. **Apply:** Use information in new situations
4. **Analyze:** Draw connections among ideas
5. **Evaluate:** Justify a decision or course of action
6. **Create:** Produce new or original work

**Source:** Yale Poorvu Center for Teaching and Learning. "Bloom's Taxonomy." https://poorvucenter.yale.edu/teaching/teaching-resource-library/blooms-taxonomy

### University of Arkansas - Using Bloom's Taxonomy

**Key Principle:** "Bloom's taxonomy is a powerful tool to help develop learning objectives because it explains the **process of learning**."

**Application:** Helps instructors appropriately align instruction to learning objectives, including planning of learning activities and delivery of assessments.

**Source:** University of Arkansas (July 26, 2022). "Using Bloom's Taxonomy to Write Effective Learning Objectives." https://tips.uark.edu/using-blooms-taxonomy/

### Measurable Verbs

**Benjamin Bloom's Contribution:** Created a taxonomy of **measurable verbs** to help describe and classify observable knowledge, skills, attitudes, behaviors, and abilities.

**Key Insight:** Verbs like "understand" or "know" are not measurable. Verbs like "demonstrate," "calculate," "design," and "evaluate" are.

**Source:** Utica University. "Bloom's Taxonomy of Measurable Verbs." https://www.utica.edu/academic/Assessment/new/Blooms%20Taxonomy%20-%20Best.pdf

### ERIC Study (2022) - Sudirtha et al.

**Study Focus:** Effectiveness of developing revised Bloom's taxonomy-oriented learning activities to improve students' metacognitive abilities

**Key Finding:** Bloom's taxonomy-oriented learning activities significantly improve students' **metacognitive abilities** and **strengths in studying and utilizing learning materials**.

**Citation:** Sudirtha, I.G., Widiana, I.W., & Adijaya, M.A. (2022). "The Effectiveness of Using Revised Bloom's Taxonomy-Oriented Learning Activities to Improve Students' Metacognitive Abilities." Journal of Education and E-Learning Research. [Cited by 52 papers]

### SSRN Study (2025) - Muhamad Sori & Wan Mustapha

**Study Focus:** Bloom's Taxonomy for Effective Teaching and Learning

**Key Finding:** Development of learning assessments based on Bloom's Taxonomy improves alignment between **learning outcomes** and the **intended objectives** of instruction.

**Citation:** Muhamad Sori, Z., & Wan Mustapha, W.H. (2025). "Bloom's Taxonomy for Effective Teaching and Learning." SSRN. [Cited by 8 papers]

## Observable and Measurable Learning Outcomes

### Michigan Tech University

**Definition:** "A learning objective is a statement that clearly and precisely describes what the learner will **know and be able to DO** by the end of the course, module or unit."

**Key Principle:** Focus on **observable actions**, not internal states.

**Source:** Michigan Tech University, Center for Teaching and Learning. "Creating measurable and observable learning objectives." https://www.mtu.edu/ctl/online-learning/course-development/learning-objectives/

### Maverick Learning (2025)

**Effective Learning Outcome Characteristics:**
- **Measurable/Observable:** Can be assessed through demonstration
- **Specific/Relevant:** Tied to real-world application
- **Succinct:** Clear and concise

**Source:** Maverick Learning Press (2025). "Writing Measurable Learning Outcomes." https://mlpp.pressbooks.pub/mavlearn/chapter/writing-measurable-learning-outcomes/

### Learning Everest (2024)

**Key Principle:** "Measurable learning objectives are the **foundation of effective course design**. Without them, instructional materials run the risk [of being ineffective]."

**Implication:** Cannot assess competence without measurable objectives.

**Source:** Learning Everest (May 10, 2024). "4 Importance of Measurable Learning Objectives." https://www.learningeverest.com/measurable-learning-objectives/

### DePaul University

**Characteristics of Effective Learning Outcomes:**
- **Measurable:** Outcomes are specific, observable, and can be assessed
- **Use concrete action verbs:** Not "understand" but "demonstrate," "calculate," "design"
- **Concise:** Clear and direct

**Source:** DePaul University Teaching Commons. "Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes." https://resources.depaul.edu/teaching-commons/teaching-guides/course-design/Pages/course-objectives-learning-outcomes.aspx

## Rubric Design and Reliability

### Moskal & Leydens (2000) - Foundational Study

**Study Focus:** Scoring rubric development: Validity and reliability

**Key Finding:** Rubrics improve both **validity** (measuring what you intend to measure) and **reliability** (consistency across raters and time).

**Purpose:** Define concepts of validity and reliability and explain how they relate to scoring rubric development.

**Citation:** Moskal, B.M., & Leydens, J.A. (2000). "Scoring rubric development: Validity and reliability." Practical Assessment, Research & Evaluation. [Cited by 1,273 papers]

### Jonsson & Svingby (2007) - Systematic Review

**Study Scope:** Systematic review of 75 studies on rubric use

**Key Findings:**
1. **Reliable scoring** of performance assessments can be enhanced by the use of rubrics
2. Rubrics improve **inter-rater reliability** (agreement between different scorers)
3. Rubrics improve **intra-rater reliability** (consistency within the same scorer over time)

**Implication:** Rubrics are not optional for defensible assessment—they are required.

**Citation:** Jonsson, A., & Svingby, G. (2007). "The use of scoring rubrics: Reliability, validity and educational consequences." Educational Research Review. [Cited by 2,461 papers]

### PMC Study (2024) - Clinical Oral Assessment

**Study Focus:** Reliability of rubrics in assessment of clinical oral presentations

**Key Finding:** Rubric assessment was found to have **high statistical significance (P < 0.0001)** when compared with traditional method of examination.

**Implication:** Rubrics significantly improve assessment reliability even in subjective domains like oral presentations.

**Citation:** PMC (July 5, 2024). "Reliability of rubrics in the assessment of clinical oral presentations." https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11392252/

### AAC&U - Inter-Rater Reliability Study

**Study Focus:** Examining design and inter-rater reliability of a rubric measuring research quality across multiple disciplines

**Key Finding:** Well-designed rubrics can maintain reliability **across disciplines**, not just within a single domain.

**Implication:** Rubric design principles are generalizable.

**Source:** AAC&U VALUE Research Hub. "Examining Design and Inter-Rater Reliability of a Rubric Measuring Research Quality Across Multiple Disciplines." https://www.aacu.org/value-research-hub/examining-design-and-inter-rater-reliability-of-a-rubric-measuring-research-quality-across-multiple-disciplines

### Advance HE (UK)

**Study Focus:** Using rubrics to improve marking reliability and to clarify good performance

**Key Findings:**
- Introduction of rubrics **raised the marks** students achieved in their second assignment
- Improved **marker reliability**, reducing inter-group variability
- Clarified expectations for students, improving performance

**Implication:** Rubrics benefit both assessors (reliability) and learners (clarity).

**Source:** Advance HE (UK). "Using rubrics to improve marking reliability and to clarify good performance." https://www.advance-he.ac.uk/knowledge-hub/using-rubrics-improve-marking-reliability-and-clarify-good-performance

### ScienceDirect Study

**Key Finding:** "Several researchers have reported that teachers' assessment is **more reliable if a rubric is used**."

**Consensus:** Rubrics improve reliability across multiple studies and contexts.

**Source:** ScienceDirect. "Reliability and validity of rubrics for assessment through writing." https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1075293510000048

### Illinois State University (2025)

**Best Practices for Rubric Reliability:**
- Use **detailed descriptors** (not vague language)
- **Pilot the rubric** with sample assignments
- **Revise based on feedback** from multiple raters
- **Share the rubric** with learners before assessment

**Source:** Illinois State University Center for Integrated Professional Development (October 24, 2025). "Creating Rubrics." https://prodev.illinoisstate.edu/instructional-resources/feedback/rubrics/

## Key Takeaways for Book

### Learning Objectives

1. **Bloom's Taxonomy Is Foundational:** 1,783 citations—differentiates between cognitive skill levels and guides objective design

2. **Measurable Verbs Required:** "Understand" is not measurable; "demonstrate," "calculate," "design," and "evaluate" are

3. **Focus on Observable Actions:** Learning objectives must describe what learners will **do**, not what they will "know" or "understand"

4. **Higher-Order Skills Matter:** Objectives should require application, analysis, evaluation, or creation—not just recall

5. **Alignment Is Critical:** Objectives must align with assessments and instructional activities

### Rubric Design

6. **Rubrics Improve Reliability:** 2,461 citations—systematic review shows rubrics enhance reliable scoring

7. **Inter-Rater Reliability:** Rubrics improve agreement between different scorers

8. **Intra-Rater Reliability:** Rubrics improve consistency within the same scorer over time

9. **Statistical Significance:** Rubric assessment shows high statistical significance (P < 0.0001) compared to traditional methods

10. **Cross-Disciplinary:** Well-designed rubrics maintain reliability across multiple disciplines

11. **Detailed Descriptors Required:** Vague rubrics fail—detailed descriptors improve reliability

12. **Pilot and Revise:** Rubrics must be tested with sample assignments and revised based on feedback

### Strategic Implications

13. **Cannot Prove Without Rubrics:** Defensible credentials require rubric-based assessment

14. **Rubrics Benefit Learners:** Clarify expectations, improve performance, reduce anxiety

15. **Rubrics Benefit Assessors:** Reduce cognitive load, improve consistency, enable defensibility

16. **Rubrics Enable Automation:** Clear rubrics can be applied by AI or human assessors consistently

17. **Proof Statements > Learning Objectives:** "Learner can [action] under [conditions] to [standard]" is more defensible than "Learner will understand [concept]"
