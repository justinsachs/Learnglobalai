# Credential Value and Performance Assessment Research

## Credential Value in Labor Market

### Brookings Institution Research (2026)

**Study Scope:** Analysis of over 156 million U.S. resumes to determine which non-degree credentials (NDCs) pay off

**Source:** Escobari, M., & Seyal, I. (2026). "The credential boom is here, but which ones actually help workers?" Brookings Institution. https://www.brookings.edu/articles/credential-boom-which-ones-help-workers/

#### Key Finding 1: Relevance Drives Return

**Job-Relevant NDCs:**
- First job-relevant NDC: **3.8% wage premium**
- Each additional job-relevant NDC: **+1.0% wage increase**

**Job-Irrelevant NDCs:**
- First job-irrelevant NDC: **1.8% wage premium** (less than half the relevant premium)
- Each additional job-irrelevant NDC: **Zero marginal return**

**Definition of Job Relevance:** Based on how concentrated a credential is within a given occupation (e.g., JavaScript certifications among software developers, project management certifications among management analysts)

**Implication:** Credentials must be tied to specific occupational competence to have labor market value. Generic credentials do not stack.

#### Key Finding 2: Credential Type Matters

**Certifications** (proctored exams, third-party validation, renewal requirements):
- Show **strong positive returns** when job-relevant
- Accumulation is rewarded (consistent with genuine skill development)
- Returns stem from rigor of assessment, renewal requirements, or employer recognition

**Badges and Certificates** (completion-based):
- Generate **one-time wage bump** from initial possession
- Additional badges or certificates add **little to no value**
- Returns look more like signaling than skill accumulation

**Implication:** Assessment rigor and ongoing verification create credential value. Completion certificates without proof do not stack.

#### Key Finding 3: NDCs Matter Most for Non-College and Early-Career Workers

**Workers Without Bachelor's Degree:**
- First job-relevant NDC: **6.8% wage premium** (nearly double the return for college graduates)

**Early-Career Workers:**
- First job-relevant NDC: **~6% wage premium**
- Each additional relevant NDC: **+2% wage increase**

**Experienced Workers:**
- Accumulation returns: **close to zero**

**Access Paradox:** Despite higher returns, non-college and early-career workers earn NDCs **less often** than college-educated and experienced workers—underscoring persistent access and information barriers.

**Implication:** Verified competence credentials have highest value for those without traditional degrees, but access remains a barrier.

### CEPR Research (2026)

**Key Finding:** A first job-relevant certification is associated with a **4.1% wage premium**, with each additional relevant certification adding another **2.2%**.

**Source:** CEPR VoxEU (January 31, 2026). "The market value of non-degree credentials: New evidence from 37 million US workers." https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/market-value-non-degree-credentials-new-evidence-37-million-us-workers

**Consistency:** Findings align with Brookings research—relevance and rigor drive value.

### Stanford GSB Research

**Study Focus:** Labor market value of credentials obtained from Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and shared on business networking platforms

**Finding:** MOOC credentials have **limited labor market value** unless tied to specific job-relevant skills and verified through rigorous assessment

**Source:** Stanford Graduate School of Business. "The Value of Non-traditional Credentials in the Labor Market." https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/value-non-traditional-credentials-labor-market

### Credential Value Index (CVI) - Burning Glass Institute

**Tool:** Free, open-access tool designed to measure and display real-world labor market value of credentials

**Purpose:** Provides transparency around which credentials lead to quality jobs (jobs that offer living wages, career advancement, and stability)

**Source:** Burning Glass Institute, Learn Work Ecosystem Library (June 17, 2025). https://learnworkecosystemlibrary.com/initiatives/credential-value-index-cvi-burning-glass-institute/

### Jobs for the Future (JFF) Position

**Principle:** "Credentials have value when they lead to quality jobs—which we define as jobs that not only offer living wages but also provide opportunities for advancement and stability."

**Problem:** Too many credentials exist without clear evidence of labor market value

**Source:** Jobs for the Future (June 5, 2025). "Too Many Credentials, Not Enough Value. Let's Change That." https://www.jff.org/blog/too-many-credentials-not-enough-value-lets-change-that/

## Performance-Based Assessment Effectiveness

### PMC Study (2010) - Fastré et al.

**Study:** Investigated effect of performance-based versus competence-based assessment criteria on task performance and self-assessment skills

**Finding:** Performance-based assessment improves both task performance and learners' ability to accurately self-assess

**Citation:** Fastré, G.M.J., et al. (2010). "The effects of performance-based assessment criteria on task performance and self-assessment skills." PMC. [Cited by 85 papers] https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2964459/

### Taylor & Francis Systematic Review (2025)

**Study:** Systematic-narrative review of performance-based assessment research

**Key Finding:** "The common goal of the various performance-based assessments is to elicit what students know **and can do**" (emphasis added)

**Implication:** Performance-based assessment focuses on demonstration of competence, not just knowledge recall

**Citation:** Taylor & Francis (September 7, 2025). "A systematic-narrative review of performance-based assessment." https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/02602938.2025.2553341

### Springer Study (2025) - Yaghoubi et al.

**Study:** The effect of performance-based assessment on academic achievement

**Key Findings:**
- PBA is particularly effective in fostering **student resilience and motivation**
- Supported by enhanced **teacher feedback**
- Leads to measurable improvements in academic performance

**Citation:** Yaghoubi, M., et al. (2025). "The effect of performance-based assessment on academic achievement." Springer. [Cited by 4 papers] https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40468-025-00385-6

### ResearchGate Study (2025)

**Study:** Effectiveness of Performance-Based Assessment Tools (PBATs) and students' academic performance

**Finding:** PBA improves problem-solving abilities and boosts confidence in studying

**Citation:** ResearchGate (August 9, 2025). "Effectiveness of Performance-Based Assessment Tools (PBATs) and the Students' Academic Performance." https://www.researchgate.net/publication/360539052

### ERIC Study (2023) - Adjei

**Study:** Impact of performance-based assessment strategies on pre-service teachers (PSTs)

**Key Finding:** PBA identified as a useful teaching strategy that improves PSTs' **problem-solving abilities** and boosts their **confidence**

**Citation:** Adjei, Y. (2023). "The impact of performance-based assessment strategies." ERIC. [Cited by 10 papers] https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1409640.pdf

### Brown University - Annenberg Institute

**Study:** Evaluation of comparability claims in local scoring of performance assessments across districts

**Focus:** Ensuring performance assessments are scored consistently and fairly across different contexts

**Finding:** Performance assessment requires careful rubric design and scorer training to maintain validity

**Source:** Annenberg Institute, Brown University. "Performance Assessment: A Deeper Look at Practice and Research." https://annenberg.brown.edu/sites/default/files/VUE47.pdf

## Assessment Validity and Reliability

### PMC Study (2023) - Gupta et al.

**Key Finding:** "Validity and reliability for students' assessment should be considered a **unified phenomenon**, a unified concept, instead of discrete units."

**Implication:** Assessment quality requires both measuring the right thing (validity) and measuring it consistently (reliability)

**Citation:** Gupta, K., et al. (2023). "Validity and Reliability of Students' Assessment." PMC. [Cited by 14 papers] https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10666833/

### Instructure (2023)

**Key Principle:** "Measuring What Matters: Validity and Reliability in Assessment"

**Validity:** Does the assessment measure what it claims to measure?

**Reliability:** Does the assessment produce consistent results across different contexts and scorers?

**Implication:** Both are essential for defensible credentials

**Source:** Instructure (September 8, 2023). "Measuring What Matters: Validity and Reliability in Assessment." https://www.instructure.com/resources/blog/measuring-what-matters-validity-and-reliability-assessment

### Marco Learning (2023)

**Key Principle:** "Validity and reliability are meaningful measurements that should be taken into account when attempting to evaluate the status of or progress of students."

**Implication:** Without valid and reliable assessments, credentials cannot be trusted

**Source:** Marco Learning (January 24, 2023). "Importance of Validity and Reliability in Classroom Assessment." https://marcolearning.com/the-two-keys-to-quality-testing-reliability-and-validity/

### Voyager Sopris (2023)

**Key Principle:** "Validity and reliability are both essential concepts when it comes to assessments. Both are similar in that they are related to the quality of assessment results."

**Implication:** Assessment quality determines credential trustworthiness

**Source:** Voyager Sopris (April 12, 2023). "Ensuring the Accuracy of Your Assessment Results." https://www.voyagersopris.com/vsl/blog/ensuring-the-accuracy-of-your-assessment-results

## Key Takeaways for Book

### Credential Value

1. **Relevance Is Everything:** Job-relevant credentials have 2x the wage premium of irrelevant credentials (3.8% vs 1.8%)

2. **Rigor Creates Value:** Certifications with proctored exams and renewal requirements show strong returns; completion certificates do not stack

3. **Stacking Only Works When Relevant:** Each additional job-relevant credential adds value; irrelevant credentials have zero marginal return

4. **Highest Value for Non-College Workers:** First job-relevant credential worth 6.8% wage premium for workers without bachelor's degree (vs. 3.8% for college grads)

5. **Early-Career Workers Benefit Most:** ~6% wage premium for first credential, +2% for each additional relevant credential

6. **Access Paradox:** Those who would benefit most (non-college, early-career) earn NDCs less often than those who benefit least

7. **1.5 Million NDCs Exist:** Credential marketplace has exploded, but most lack clear evidence of value

### Performance-Based Assessment

8. **PBA Improves Outcomes:** Consistent research showing performance-based assessment improves task performance, problem-solving, resilience, and confidence

9. **"Know and Can Do":** Performance assessment focuses on demonstration, not just recall

10. **Feedback Is Critical:** PBA effectiveness depends on quality of teacher feedback and rubric clarity

11. **Validity + Reliability = Trust:** Both must be present for defensible credentials

12. **Comparability Requires Discipline:** Performance assessments must be scored consistently across contexts to maintain trust

### Strategic Implications

13. **Content Is Not the Moat:** In a world of abundant content, the moat is **verified competence**

14. **Proof Must Be Defensible:** Assessment rigor, rubric clarity, and evidence artifacts determine credential value

15. **Renewal Creates Trust:** Credentials that require re-verification maintain value; one-time credentials decay

16. **Transparency Matters:** Employers and learners need visibility into what credentials actually measure and predict
