# The Movement Playbook: How to Change the World

This book is not just a book. It is a manifesto.

It is a call to action. It is a declaration of war on the industrialized model of education that is failing our learners, our organizations, and our society.

But a manifesto is not enough. A good idea is not enough. To change the world, you need a **movement**.[^1]

This chapter is the playbook for that movement. It is a guide for how you can take the ideas in this book and use them to start a revolution in your own organization, in your own community, and in the world.

## A Movement, Not a Mandate

You cannot force people to change. You cannot mandate a new way of thinking. You have to **inspire them to want to change**.

This is the fundamental difference between a top-down mandate and a bottom-up movement.

A mandate is about compliance. A movement is about commitment.

A mandate is about control. A movement is about empowerment.

A mandate can change behavior. But only a movement can change hearts and minds.

## The Anatomy of a Movement

Every successful movement has three key ingredients:[^2]

### 1. A Clear and Compelling Vision

A movement needs a **story**. It needs a clear and compelling vision of the future that people can believe in.

The vision of the Adaptive Education™ movement is simple: **a world where every person has the opportunity to achieve their full potential**.

It is a world where learning is personalized, not standardized. Where competence is valued more than compliance. Where learners are empowered, not controlled.

This is a vision worth fighting for.

### 2. A Passionate, Committed Core

Every movement starts with a **small group of passionate, committed people**—the early adopters.[^3]

These are the people who “get it.” They are the ones who are willing to take a risk, to challenge the status quo, and to lead the charge.

Your first job as a movement builder is to **find your early adopters**. Find the people who share your passion and your vision. Find the people who are willing to join you in the trenches.

### 3. A Community of Practice

A movement needs a **home**. It needs a place where people can come together to learn from each other, to support each other, and to co-create the future.

This is the role of a **community of practice**.[^4]

A community of practice is a group of people who share a common interest and who are committed to learning from each other. It is a place where you can ask questions, share your successes and failures, and get the support you need to keep going when things get tough.

## The Playbook: How to Start Your Own Movement

So how do you do it? How do you start a movement to bring Adaptive Education™ to your organization?

Here is a simple, four-step playbook:

### Step 1: Find Your People

Start by finding **one or two other people** in your organization who share your passion for this idea. Have a conversation. Share this book. Start a book club.

This is your core team. This is the beginning of your movement.

### Step 2: Start a Pilot Project

Don’t try to change the whole organization at once. Start with a **small, manageable pilot project**.

Pick a single course or a single program. Use the ADAPT framework to transform it into an adaptive learning experience.

Measure the results. Show the impact. Create a success story that you can use to win over new supporters.

### Step 3: Build a Community of Practice

As you start to have success, you will attract more and more people to your cause. This is the time to build a **community of practice**.

-   Start a regular meeting—a lunch and learn, a brown bag, a virtual happy hour.
-   Create a space for people to share their work and to learn from each other—a Slack channel, a Microsoft Teams group, a shared folder.
-   Celebrate your successes. Recognize and reward the people who are leading the charge.

This is how you build momentum. This is how you turn a small group of passionate people into a powerful force for change.

### Step 4: Scale the Movement

As your community of practice grows, you will reach a tipping point. The movement will start to take on a life of its own.

This is the time to **scale the movement**. This is the time to go from a single pilot project to a full-scale transformation.

-   Develop a formal training program to teach people how to build adaptive learning experiences.
-   Create a center of excellence to provide support and guidance to the rest of the organization.
-   Work with senior leaders to make Adaptive Education™ a strategic priority for the entire organization.

This is how you change the world. One learner, one course, one organization at a time.

## Failure Modes

-   **Trying to go it alone** — the lone wolf
-   **Lack of a clear vision** — the “what are we even doing here?” problem
-   **Failure to build a community** — the “Field of Dreams” fallacy
-   **Giving up too soon** — the marathon, not a sprint

## Operator Playbook: Your First Movement-Building Action

**Step 1: Identify one person** in your network who you think would be receptive to the ideas in this book.

**Step 2: Send them a copy of the book** (or a link to it).

**Step 3: Ask them to have a conversation with you** about it.

**Step 4: Listen.**
-   What resonates with them?
-   What are their questions?
-   What are their concerns?

**Step 5: Plant the seed.**
-   “What if we tried to do something like this here?”

## Proof Task

**Step 1: Find a community of practice** that is focused on a topic you are passionate about.
-   It could be a local meetup group, an online forum, a professional association.

**Step 2: Join the community.**
-   Attend a meeting. Introduce yourself. Start listening.

**Step 3: Contribute.**
-   Ask a question. Share a resource. Offer to help.

**Step 4: Reflect on the power of community.**
-   What did you learn?
-   How did it feel to be part of a group of people who share your passion?
-   How can you apply what you have learned to building your own movement?
