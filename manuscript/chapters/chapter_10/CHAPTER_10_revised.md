# CHAPTER 10: The Transfer Playbook

## 1. Define the Transfer Target

*   **Identify the real-world performance context.** Where will the learner apply this skill? What are the conditions, constraints, and pressures?
*   **Determine the required transfer distance.** Is it near, moderate, or far transfer? Be honest about the novelty and variability of the real-world environment.
*   **Define the evidence artifact.** What will the learner produce as proof of competence? This artifact is the target of the entire learning process.

## 2. Design the Transfer Task

*   **Create a novel scenario.** The scenario must be one the learner has not practiced. It should be realistic and include the same level of ambiguity and complexity as the real world.
*   **Define the observable performance.** What must the learner do to demonstrate competence? The task should be a direct reflection of the real-world performance.
*   **Develop a clear rubric.** The rubric should define the criteria for success, including critical errors, pass/fail thresholds, and indicators of mastery.

## 3. Design the Learning Experience

*   **Start with the transfer task.** The transfer task is the end goal. The learning experience should be designed backward from there.
*   **Provide a scaffolded series of practice opportunities.** Start with near-transfer tasks and gradually increase the transfer distance.
*   **Provide targeted feedback.** The feedback should be focused on helping the learner improve their performance on the transfer task.

## 4. Build the Evidence Portfolio

*   **Collect the evidence artifacts.** Every performance on a transfer task should be captured as an evidence artifact.
*   **Link the artifacts to competencies.** Each artifact should be linked to a specific competency in the Competence Ledger.
*   **Include context and verification.** The portfolio should include metadata about the context of the performance and a verification that transfer occurred.

## 5. Iterate and Improve

*   **Analyze the transfer data.** Are learners successfully transferring the skill to the real world? Where are they struggling?
*   **Use the data to improve the learning experience.** The transfer data is the most valuable feedback you have. Use it to improve the design of the learning experience and the transfer tasks.
