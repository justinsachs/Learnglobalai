## The Steelman: The Enduring Value of a Liberal Arts Education

**The Argument:** The relentless focus on skills, on competence, on the immediate and the practical, is a dangerous and short-sighted path. It is a path that will lead to a world of hyper-specialized, technically proficient, but ultimately uneducated people. It is a path that will lead to a world without a soul.

A true education is not about acquiring a set of skills. It is about cultivating a set of sensibilities. It is about learning how to think, not what to think. It is about learning how to see the world through a variety of lenses, how to appreciate the beauty and the complexity of the human experience, and how to grapple with the great questions of meaning and purpose that have animated human civilization for millennia.

This is the enduring value of a liberal arts education. It is an education that is not about preparing you for your first job, but for your last. It is an education that is not about making a living, but about making a life.

To reduce education to a mere instrument of economic utility is to do a profound disservice to the human spirit. It is to trade our birthright for a mess of pottage. It is to create a world that is efficient, but not a world that is worth living in.


## Case Study: Amarillo College and the Future of Personalized Learning

Amarillo College, a community college in Texas, is a powerful example of what the future of learning can look like. Faced with a diverse student body, including a large number of adult learners and students pursuing their GED, the college recognized that the traditional, one-size-fits-all model of education was failing to meet the needs of its students.

**A Focus on the Underserved**

Instead of starting with the traditional student, Amarillo College focused on the students who were being left behind. They worked with adaptive learning publishers to develop new courseware that was specifically designed for adult learners and students in their GED program. This was a radical departure from the traditional approach, which has long been to force these students to adapt to a system that was not designed for them.

**Personalized Pathways to Success**

The college used adaptive courseware to create personalized pathways for each student. Students who were struggling with a particular concept could get the extra support they needed, while students who were ready to move on could accelerate their learning. This allowed every student to learn at their own pace, and to get the specific help they needed to be successful.

**The Results: A More Equitable and Effective System**

The results of this experiment were dramatic. Students who had been disengaged from the traditional classroom were now motivated and successful. Students who had been struggling were now confident and capable. And the college as a whole was able to serve a more diverse student body more effectively and more equitably.

The Amarillo College story is a powerful reminder that the future of learning is not about technology; it is about people. It is about creating a system that is designed to meet the needs of every learner, not just the average learner. It is about creating a system that is personalized, that is competence-centric, and that is equitable. It is about creating a system that is worthy of the learners it serves.
