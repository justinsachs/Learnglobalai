# The Individual Playbook: Your Personal Adaptive Education Loop

For most of history, education has been something that was done *to* you.

You went to school. You sat in a classroom. A teacher told you what to learn.

That world is over.

In the new economy, the pace of change is too fast, the half-life of skills is too short, and the opportunities for learning are too abundant to wait for someone else to teach you what you need to know.

**You have to become the CEO of your own learning.**[^1]

You have to take ownership of your own development. You have to become a **self-directed, lifelong learner**.[^2]

This chapter is your playbook for doing just that. It is about how you can apply the principles of Adaptive Education™ to your own life, to create a **personal adaptive learning loop** that will keep you learning and growing for the rest of your career.

## The ADAPT Framework for Individuals

The same ADAPT framework that organizations use to build adaptive learning programs can be used by individuals to manage their own learning.

It is a simple, five-step process for taking control of your own development:

### 1. Audit: What Do I Need to Learn?

The first step is to get clear on what you need to learn. This means doing a personal **skills audit**.

- **What are your career goals?**
- **What skills do you need to achieve those goals?**
- **What are your current skill gaps?**

Be honest with yourself. Where are you strong? Where are you weak? What do you need to learn to get to the next level?

### 2. Define: What Does Success Look Like?

Once you know what you need to learn, you need to define what success looks like. This means creating a **personal proof statement**.

- **What will you be able to do** once you have learned this skill?
- **How will you prove it?**
- **What is the project you will complete, the thing you will build, the problem you will solve?**

This is your personal proof task. It is the tangible outcome that will show the world (and yourself) that you have mastered the skill.

### 3. Assemble: Build Your Personal SourcePack

You cannot learn from the entire internet. You need to curate your own **personal SourcePack** of trusted resources.

- **Who are the experts** you trust on this topic?
- **What are the books, blogs, podcasts, and courses** that you will use as your source of truth?
- **Who are the people** in your Personal Learning Network (PLN) that you can turn to for help and advice? [^3]

This is your personal library. It is the foundation of your learning.

### 4. Personalize: Design Your Learning Path

Now it is time to design your learning path. This is your personal curriculum.

- **What is the most efficient path** to get you from where you are to where you want to be?
- **What are the key concepts** you need to master?
- **What are the practice exercises** you will do to build your skills?

This is not a rigid plan. It is a flexible roadmap that you will adjust as you go.

### 5. Transfer: Apply Your Learning in the Real World

The final step is to **transfer your learning to the real world**.

- **Do your proof task.** Build the thing. Solve the problem. Create the proof.
- **Share your work.** Get feedback from your PLN.
- **Reflect on your learning.** What did you learn? What could you do better next time?

This is the loop. Audit, Define, Assemble, Personalize, Transfer. And then you do it again, for the next skill you need to learn.

## The Habits of a Lifelong Learner

This is not just a process. It is a **mindset**. It is a set of habits that you can cultivate to become a more effective lifelong learner.[^4]

- **Be curious:** Ask questions. Explore new ideas. Never stop learning.
- **Read voraciously:** Books, articles, blogs, code. The more you read, the more you know.
- **Build a PLN:** You cannot learn alone. You need a network of people who can support you, challenge you, and help you to grow.[^5]
- **Embrace the struggle:** Learning is hard. It is supposed to be. If it is not hard, you are not learning.
- **Create, don’t just consume:** The best way to learn something is to try to build it, to explain it, to teach it to someone else.

This is the work of a lifetime. It is the work of becoming a **self-directed, adaptive learner**.

## Failure Modes

- **“I don’t have time to learn”** — the excuse of the busy professional
- **Passive consumption** — reading and watching without doing
- **Learning in a vacuum** — failing to build a PLN
- **Lack of a clear goal** — learning without a purpose

## Operator Playbook: Your First Personal ADAPT Loop

**Step 1: Pick one skill you want to learn in the next 90 days**

**Step 2: Define your proof task**
- What will you create to prove you have learned this skill?

**Step 3: Assemble your SourcePack**
- What are the 3-5 key resources you will use to learn this skill?

**Step 4: Design your learning path**
- What are the key things you need to learn, in what order?
- What are the practice exercises you will do?

**Step 5: Schedule your learning**
- Block out time on your calendar for learning, just like you would for any other important project.

---

## Proof Task

**Step 1: Build your Personal Learning Network (PLN)**
- Identify 5 people you can learn from on this topic.
- Follow them on Twitter or LinkedIn.
- Read their blogs. Listen to their podcasts.

**Step 2: Reach out to one of them**
- Ask them a smart question.
- Share something you have learned.
- Start a conversation.

**Step 3: Reflect on the power of your network**
- How can your PLN help you to learn faster and more effectively?
- How can you contribute to the learning of others in your network?
